Urban Residential Nighttime Thermal Comfort
under Climate Change

Code and data for reproducing the paper. 6 Chinese cities, 7 climate scenarios, SET-based overheating risk analysis with building-energy-model validation.

Input Data Packages

EPW Weather Files

42 files, 64 MB
6 cities × 7 scenarios (2020 + 2040/2060 × RCP 2.6/4.5/8.5)
Used by: Step 1, Figure 2-b, 3-a, 4-r, 6-c

Building Energy Excel

6 files, 122 MB
Per-building annual cooling energy (kWh) for 6 strategies
Used by: Figure 6-a, 6-b

Energy Capacity Summary

1 CSV, 1.8 MB
Per-building capacity (W) and energy (kWh) by strategy & scenario
Used by: Figure 6-b

GW Load Curve Caches

6 pickle files, 1.2 MB
City-wide GW-scale load curves by date & strategy
Used by: Figure 6-c

Grid Load Data

1 Excel, <1 MB
Provincial 24h electricity load profiles (reference curves)
Used by: Figure 6-c

Shape Coefficient

2 Excel files, 31 MB
Building footprint, height, shape coefficient, age (GZ + SZ)
Used by: Figure 3-c

China Standard Maps

20 SHP files, 37 MB
County / city / nine-dash line boundaries (GS 2024-0650)
Used by: Figure 2-c, 2-d, 5 (original), fig2-1

Beijing Weibo Data

1 CSV, 327 KB
Anonymised summer check-in records (one reference user intact)
Used by: Figure 2-c

Cluster Maps

6 CSV, 67 MB
BuildingID → LandNum, Cluster, Fnum assignments
Used by: Step 1, Figure 2-b, 4-r

GeoJSON Grid Data

64 files, 7 MB
Pre-computed anonymised grid (from private SHP)
Used by: Figure 2-c/d/5 public scripts
